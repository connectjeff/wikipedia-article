## Draft Article Quality Summary

Report run: June 9, 2026. Reviewed inputs: `drafts/article_draft.wikitext`, `drafts/outline_and_justification.md`, `afc/submission_guide.md`, `sources/source_dossier.md`, and the current 79-row source dossier.

Overall AfC readiness: moderate and suitable for a good-faith as-is submission after human editorial review. The draft is short, neutral, and sourced, which is appropriate for a COI-disclosed AfC submission. The main weakness remains notability, not verifiability: the best sources document Alderson's role in larger education-technology projects rather than providing sustained biographical profile coverage. The draft includes one compact paragraph using `SCH1`, `PESCNEWS2013`, and NCES2013 to connect Alderson to ConnectEDU, PESC-adjacent student-record exchange, and MyData/MyDataButton without expanding into a resume-like list of PESC roles.

Lead: fair. The lead accurately summarizes the strongest supported roles: education technology, Air Force service, Eduventures, MATLAB Grader, inBloom, and Natick town moderator. It should not be expanded unless a stronger independent profile source is found. Adding PESC/MyData detail to the lead would overweight supporting evidence.

Education and military service: acceptable but source-limited. The section is concise and uses a local oral-history/archive source for WPI and military-service facts and a MathWorks speaker bio for the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign Master of Education item. Private WPI and UIUC transcript PDFs are archived in `archive/private_records/` for internal corroboration only and should not be cited, uploaded, or submitted as AfC source evidence. Because the published sources are primary-adjacent or affiliated, this section should remain brief and should not be used to carry notability.

Education technology career: strongest section. Data & Society is the core Alderson-specific source because it identifies his technical role in the New York implementation of inBloom and includes attributed commentary. SXSW EDU and Education Week provide context for why inBloom mattered, but they do not mention Alderson. Campus Technology provides independent trade coverage of his Eduventures analyst work. MIT Open Learning supports the MathWorks/MATLAB Grader connection but is event-bio context rather than profile coverage. `SCH1` adds independent brief support for Alderson's ConnectEDU role in PESC-adjacent standards-based student-record exchange discussions. `PESCNEWS2013` and NCES2013 strengthen narrow MyData/PESC verification. PESC2012, ERUG1, board listings, awards, and implementation-guide contributor records should remain dossier support unless a reviewer asks for more context.

Municipal service: verifiable but low notability weight. Natick Report supports the 2025 moderator transition and Hybrid Town Meeting Committee activity. These are useful local public-role facts, but routine election and meeting coverage is unlikely to carry a standalone biography by itself.

References and source mix: good source hygiene, moderate notability risk. The draft uses 14 named references and emphasizes the strongest independent or directly relevant published sources while avoiding most primary/affiliated source clutter. It correctly excludes private transcripts, source-dossier files, blocked pages, campaign/social-media material, legal filing details, patent-index leads, and unverified items such as `FAW1`. The reference list is appropriate for AfC review, but a reviewer may still ask for more independent significant coverage directly about Alderson.

AfC proposal materials: good process posture. The submission note clearly discloses that the subject is submitting through AfC because of a conflict of interest, and the dossier gives a transparent source-quality trail for internal review. The submission guide now directs use of source editing, draft-space submission, COI disclosure, AfC review, and a human editorial pass before saving the draft on Wikipedia. The proposal should be presented as a good-faith draft for independent review, not as a guaranteed notability case.

## Important Caveat

The current package supports a good-faith AfC submission, but acceptance is not assured. The main risk is not source verification; it is whether reviewers consider the available independent coverage sufficiently significant for a standalone biography.
