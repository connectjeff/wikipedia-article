$7M override is Natick's top issue in March 25 election. What else is on the ballot?

Tom Benoit
The MetroWest Daily News
March 24, 2025, 4:46 a.m. ET
  • Natick voters will decide March 25 on a $7 million override ballot question that would increase property taxes to fund town services.
  • There are also contested races for Select Board and School Committee, with two seats open on each panel.
  • Polls will be open from 7 a.m. to 8 p.m. on Tuesday at four locations throughout town.

NATICK — Voters will decide on a $7 million override ballot question as well as several town offices during this year's town election on Tuesday.

Last month, Select Board member Bruce Evans told the Daily News that the $7 million override is being proposed because the town's revenues are being outpaced by extenuating factors such as inflation, student needs that were exacerbated by COVID-19 and expiring one-time COVID relief funds. The Select Board unanimously approved putting the question on the ballot.

If approved, the override would increase property tax bills beginning July 1, the start of fiscal 2026. According to the town's override web page, property tax bills would have an average increase of $453 a year, or $38 a month. That's based on Natick's median single-family home value, which is $780,400.

Should the override fail, town departments would experience permanent reductions, including those already slated for the next the fiscal year. Town officials have said that 48 positions would be cut across town departments, including in Natick Public Schools. Up to 41 would be in the schools, with seven from municipal departments.

Races for Select Board, School Committee, Town Meeting reps

The Select Board and the School Committee each have competitive races.

Incumbent Select Board candidates Kathryn Coughlin, the current chair, and Bruce Evans are being challenged by Kathryn Kelly for two spots on the town's top governing board. Coughlin and Evans were each elected in 2022 and are seeking second three year-terms.

According to her website, Kelly was born in Natick before moving to California in high school and returning to the town in 2020. Kelly says she has worked for 30 years with leading tech companies, including Yahoo, Meta, Roku and Databricks.

Four people are vying for three available spots on the town's School Committee. Incumbents Cathi Collins and Elise Gorseth are being challenged by former School Committee member Donna McKenzie and former state representative hopeful Jaymin Patel.

Lastly, some precincts have competitive races for Town Meeting representatives.

Natick uses a representative town meeting format, meaning voters in 10 separate precincts decide who represents them at Town Meeting.

Precinct 2 has seven candidates running for six seats. Precinct 4 has nine candidates, also for six seats. Precinct 7 has nine candidates running for seven seats and Precinct 10 has seven candidates running for six seats.

Annual Town Election has several uncontested races

Along with the contested races, some candidates are running unopposed. Those include:

  • Planning Board, five year term: Alan Cloutier
  • Recreation and Parks Commission, two three-year terms: Incumbents David Ordway and Jessica Ordway
  • Board of Assessors, three years: Incumbent Paul Griesmer
  • Board of Health, three years: Incumbent Donald Breda
  • Constables, six three-year terms: Incumbents Kevin Flynn, Todd Gillenwater and Paul Gorman, and newcomer Frank Foss, the outgoing town moderator.
  • Town Moderator, three years: Jeffrey Alderson

Foss is stepping down as moderator after serving Natick in that capacity for 18 years.

There are no candidates for a one-year term on the Recreation and Parks Commission; a five-year term on the Natick Housing Authority; and two constable seats.

Where do I vote in Natick?

Natick, which has 25,878 registered voters, has four voting locations. Voting locations for each precinct are as follows:

  • Precincts 1, 2, 3: Kennedy Middle School (165 Mill St.)
  • Precinct 4: Morse Institute Library (13 East Central St.)
  • Precincts 5 and 6: Wilson Middle School (22 Rutledge Road)
  • Precinct 7, 8, 9, 10: Community Senior Center (117 East Central St.)

Polls will be open at 7 a.m. to 8 p.m. on Tuesday.
