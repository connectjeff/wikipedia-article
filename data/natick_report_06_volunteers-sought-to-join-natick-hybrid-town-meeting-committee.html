Volunteers sought to join Natick Hybrid Town Meeting Committee

November 20, 2025 by Bob Brown

Natick Town Moderator Jeff Alderson is seeking Town Meeting members willing to apply for the new Hybrid Town Meeting Committee. The moderator will appoint eight Town Meeting members and one Natick Pegasus rep to the group for 6-month terms.

Once the committee is appointed, work will begin, and the body must submit a report to Spring Annual Town Meeting. Town Meeting is Natick’s legislative body and members are elected.

Fall Annual Town Meeting voted favorably to start such a committee. During the Finance Committee’s review of the article on this topic, it reported that the moderator said that the earliest hybrid town meeting could start would be 2026 Fall Annual Town Meeting, but that later was possible and may be likely.

Currently, Natick Town Meeting members must attend in person, and attendance leaves much to be desired. The hope is that a hybrid Town Meeting, taking advantage of the latest technology, would allow a mix of in-person and remote attendance, potentially boosting overall attendance and making Town Meeting participation more possible for those who can’t attend in person for various reasons (Town Meeting was held remotely in 2020 during the pandemic).

The committee will study and recommend a framework for the conduct of hybrid town meetings in Natick, and address any proposed bylaw amendments. This will include looking at how other communities handle this, reviewing technologies, examining potential costs, and more.

Oh, and unlike Town Meeting currently, this committee fittingly enough could meet in hybrid fashion.
