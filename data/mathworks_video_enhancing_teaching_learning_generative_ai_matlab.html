Enhancing Teaching and Learning with MATLAB Copilot and Simulink Copilot

Overview

Join the webinar to learn how educators use generative AI tools, like MATLAB Copilot and Simulink Copilot, to create engaging curriculum.  Attendees will see real curriculum examples and workflows for creating instructional content, hands-on activities, and assessment instruments.  Educators will learn how to use these tools in their own curriculum development, as well as how to equip students with tools to aid their coding and project-based learning. We will share insightful customer stories that demonstrate the practical application of these technologies in teaching. 

Who Should Attend:

  • Instructional designers and curriculum developers 
  • University faculty and instructors in STEM disciplines 
  • Academic deans and department chairs

Highlights

In this session, you’ll learn how to: 

  • Create MATLAB and Simulink learning activities using MATLAB Copilot and Simulink Copilot  
  • Update existing learning activities to have more MATLAB interactions 
  • Use MATLAB Copilot to give students feedback, ask questions about their code, and explain errors 
  • Use Simulink Copilot to explain how complex models work and get design guidance
  • Call LLMs from within MATLAB using APIs 
  • Access MATLAB from within other Generative AI tools, like our custom GPT for ChatGPT 
  • Set proper expectations or students on responsible use of Generative AI

About the Presenters

Jeff Alderson has spent his entire career working on technology that empowers students, families, and educators to further their education and career, including those with special needs. Jeff's current vocation is Education Product Manager at MathWorks in Natick, MA, where he leads the company’s marketing efforts in teaching and learning, including the products MATLAB Grader and MATLAB Course Designer. Prior to joining MathWorks, Jeff was Principal Analyst for Technology at Eduventures, covering the emerging EdTech market in higher ed. Jeff has over 20 years of experience in deploying secure, standards-based, data solutions for education and government, as well as five years of service as a commissioned officer in the U.S. Air Force. Mr. Alderson received his B.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from WPI in Massachusetts and his M.Ed. in Education Policy, Organization & Leadership from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. 

Dr. Will Greenwood is a technical program manager at MathWorks. He supports programs in the Education Marketing organization related to curriculum development, hardware connectivity, and generative AI. Prior to joining MathWorks, Will was an Assistant Professor of Civil and Environmental Engineering at San Jose State University. Will received his PhD in Civil Engineering from the University of Michigan Ann Arbor specializing in geotechnical earthquake engineering.
