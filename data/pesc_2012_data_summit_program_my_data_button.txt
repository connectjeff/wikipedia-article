PESC | Yesterday, Today & Tomorrow | Michael D. Sessa, President & CEO, PESC 
PESC President & CEO Michael Sessa will reflect on the growth of PESC and its 15th Year Anniversary and roll 
out PESC’s updated business plan PESC 3.0 | Unifying the Education Domain Around Interoperability to 
Improve Student Achievement, which includes a number of major updates and announcement including a 
new name for the organization. 
Canadian PESC User Group | Bill McKee, Director of Operations, OCAS 
Panel 1:  Why Data Standards and How PESC Can Help 
- Kelly McMullen, ApplyAlberta 
- Trudy Sykes, OUAC 
- Jam Hamidi, BCCampus 
Panel 2: Prerequisites for Success and the Value of Co-opetition 
- Dave Mathews, Ellucian 
- Dave Moldoff, AcademyOne 
MyDataButton | Jeffrey Alderson, Senior Director of Product Innovation, ConnectEDU 
PESC Board Member Jeffrey Alderson will discuss the role he and ConnectEDU are playing in this joint White 
House/US Department of Education initiative to ensure students have access to their data. 
US Department of Education & NIEM | Michael Fillinich, Sr. Manager, OCIO, FSA 
Mike Fillinich of FSA will discuss the value of the National Information Exchange Model (NIEM) and how the 
US Department of Education and PESC will be migrating their technical strategy to NIEM.  With a number of 
free tools and resources now being used cross federal agencies, NIEM sets a much needed hierarchy for 
Education’s technical approach to standards. 
FSA Strategy for E-Authentication | Richard Gordon, CIO, FSA 
FSA CIO Richard Gordon will discuss how FSA meets the needs of the community and of students while 
ensuring accurate access through authentication.  Participation in certain federations and FSA’s overall 
strategy and the factors that need to be considered will be reviewed.  
Keynote | | David Wrate, Director of Citizen Engagement, Government of British Columbia 
David Wrate is the Director of Citizen Engagement for the Government of the Province of British Columbia. 
Actively engaged in open data for the past two years, he has been in the thick of developing and growing 
DataBC, the Provinces’ open data program.  Watching the internet evolve from Usenet groups to the web to 
mobile applications has given David a deep appreciation for the societal effects of technology.  He is an 
advocate of our lives having ‘just’ enough technology to help us be a better society. 
Electronic Transcript Exchange | Matthew Pittinsky, Ph.D., Parchment, CEO 
Parchment CEO Matthew Pittinsky, Ph.D., will discuss the results of a recent white paper that studied the 
value and effects of electronic processing with focus on transcripts and their exchange among various 
stakeholders in the education community. 
AACRAO | Michael Riley, Executive Director, AACRAO 
AACRAO’s new Executive Director Michael Riley will discuss AACRAO’s perspective on electronic processing, 
on standards, and on trends and future opportunities that may exist between AACRAO and PESC.  Being 
PESC’s strongest strategic partner, AACRAO and PESC continue to collaborate and share innovative ideas.

CommIT | Charles Leonhardt, CTO, Georgetown University 
Born out of PESC’s eAuthentication/eAuthorization (EA2) Task Force and forged through a strong 
partnership between PESC’s EA2 and Internet2’s InCommon, the Common Identity & Trust (CommIT) 
Collaborative seeks to bring easier yet more efficient sign-on functionality to teachers, professors, 
administrators, students and providers. 
CDS Task Force | Tim Calhoon, Technology Center Director, California Community Colleges 
The Common Data Services (CDS) Task Force is looking to establish an education-wide data exchange 
protocol so that any provider using PESC Approved Standards and registered in the service network could 
communicate directly with the appropriate exchange host for a targeted institution.  These services would 
be ‘payload agnostic’ and while the immediate interest is for transcript exchange, the network could be used 
for the exchange of other existing or future PESC standards and/or transactions. 
PESC Workgroup | Academic ePortfolio, ERUG, Student Loan Data Reporting, CCB, TAB 
The Academic ePortfolio and Student Loan Data Reporting Development Workgroups are focused on 
developing XML version 1.0 of the standard for their respective topic.  In addition to agreeing on the data 
elements to be included, Workgroups must produce the XML schema required for the standard along with a 
corresponding Implementation Guide and Instance Diagram.  The Education Record User Group (ERUG) 
governs the maintenance and outreach for all PESC Approved Standards in the Registrar’s and Admissions 
Officer’s sectors (eg, Transcripts, Test Scores, Admissions Application, etc.).  The Change Control Board (CCB) 
governs what becomes a PESC Approved Standard while the Technical Advisory Board (TAB) provides 
technical assistance to all workgroups and development efforts. 
Student Loan Data Reporting Workgroup
The Student Loan Data Reporting Workgroup is focusing on the development of XML version 1.0 of a 
standard that can be used by lenders, servicers, guarantors, colleges and universities to exchange student 
loan repayment data and information. 
Canadian PESC User Group
The Canadian PESC User Group includes stakeholders with Canadian-specific interests.  The Group 
represents Canadian interests in PESC to maintain ‘one standard’ and to ensure that PESC products and 
services have value to all organizations in the United States and Canada.
Student Aid User Group
The Student Aid User Group includes stakeholders with student aid-specific interests.  The Group monitors 
activities in the community to ensure interoperability and compliance with applicable statute. 
Education Record User Group
The Education Record User Group (ERUG) governs the maintenance and outreach for all PESC Approved 
Standards in the Registrar’s and Admissions Officer’s sectors (eg, Transcripts, Test Scores, Admissions 
Application, etc.).  
CEDS User Group
The Common Education Data Standards (CEDS) User Group includes stakeholders with longitudinal student 
data-specific interests.  The Group works with NCES and all other organizations to ensure communication, 
collaboration and continued support of CEDS.
Joint CCB/TAB 
The Change Control Board (CCB) governs what becomes a PESC Approved Standard while the Technical 
Advisory Board (TAB) provides technical assistance to all workgroups and development efforts.
