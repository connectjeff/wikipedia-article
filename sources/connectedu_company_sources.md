# ConnectEDU Company Source Review for Jeffrey Alderson

Scope: ConnectEDU company-background sources reviewed for the Jeffrey Alderson AfC proposal.

## Bottom Line

The Startup Intros ConnectEDU page is useful as a readable company-background summary. It describes ConnectEDU as a Boston-based education-technology company founded in 2002, with products and services spanning K-12, college, and career planning. It also summarizes funding, scale, bankruptcy, and asset-sale context. The Campus Technology 2007 article provides stronger independent trade-publication context for ConnectEDU's transcript-exchange and college-planning platform work, but it does not name Alderson. The Fortnightly Mailing / Jim Farmer guest contribution directly names Alderson with ConnectEdu at a PESC-hosted Washington briefing and summarizes his comments about electronic exchange among servicers and standards-based connections to colleges and universities. The February 2013 PESC/Yumpu source directly lists Alderson as Senior Director of Product Innovation at ConnectEDU for `MyData`.

The SlideServe presentation is useful only as a narrow ConnectEDU-era professional-context source. The original SlideServe page shell was blocked from direct local fetching, but the individual slide images were downloaded and reviewed. Slide 1 directly verifies the ConnectEDU-branded presentation title, Alderson's role as Director of Pre-Sales Solutions, a ConnectEDU email address, and the April 8, 2009 date.

Patent-specific ConnectEDU sources are tracked separately in `sources/patent_sources.md`. The current Google Scholar and Justia patent leads are useful only for patent metadata and should not be treated as independent biographical coverage.
